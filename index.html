For Clients

When a legal problem becomes personal, strategy matters.

Litigation is not only about filing a case. It is about understanding the documents, identifying the legal right, choosing the correct remedy, drafting clearly, and taking procedural steps at the right time. A weak legal strategy can turn even a genuine claim into a difficult fight.

My focus is to help clients understand where they stand legally, what documents matter, what remedy may be available, and how their matter can be approached through notices, negotiation, civil proceedings, injunctions, or other appropriate legal steps.

What clients can expect

Direct communication, careful review of documents, honest assessment of risks, and legal work prepared with seriousness. No false drama. No unnecessary complexity. No promises that law itself does not support.

Academic Research

Financial law scholarship behind the litigation practice.

Alongside civil litigation, my academic work focuses on banking law, financial regulation, capital markets, regulatory design, and the relationship between legal rules and institutional behaviour.

Financial Regulation

The Secondary Competitiveness and Growth Objective in UK Financial Regulation

Research on whether the UK’s post-FSMA 2023 competitiveness mandate risks encouraging deregulatory drift within financial regulation, especially through the regulatory behaviour of the PRA and FCA.

Banking Law

Banking Regulation After the Global Financial Crisis

Analysis of prudential regulation, supervisory objectives, Basel standards, and the post-crisis balance between financial stability, institutional resilience, competitiveness, and growth.

Capital Markets

Misleading Financial Information and Capital Markets

Critical engagement with private and public law mechanisms addressing false or misleading information in financial markets, including issuer liability, disclosure standards, and enforcement limits.

Structured Finance

Securitisation and Financial Alchemy

Work on the benefits, risks, and regulatory challenges of securitisation, including SPVs, tranching, liquidity creation, systemic-risk concerns, and post-crisis reforms.

Pro Bono Work

Open to selected pro bono matters.

I am open to taking selected pro bono or reduced-fee matters, especially where the issue involves genuine hardship, access to justice, unlawful administrative conduct, basic civil rights, documentation barriers, or public-interest concerns.

Cases involving genuine inability to afford legal assistance may be considered.
Priority may be given to matters involving documentation, civil rights, public authorities, or urgent legal harm.
Pro bono support depends on time, urgency, merits of the matter, and available capacity.
Initial contact should clearly explain the facts, documents available, urgency, and the help required.
